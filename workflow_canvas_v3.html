AI Workflow Canvas

Map your workflow. Export it. Build it.

1
What do you want to use AI for?
One sentence. Be specific enough that someone else would know exactly what this workflow does.
2
What kicks this off?
What is the starting point — a phrase you type, a day of the week, a specific event or situation?
3
What raw material does AI need?
List the specific inputs — data, documents, context, examples. Be precise, not general.
Knowledge source: If any of these inputs already live somewhere connected to your AI tool — like Notion, Salesforce, Slack, Linear, or Google Drive — specify that here. If you'll need to provide them manually each time, note that too.
4
What should AI do — and how will you check it?
Write the instructions you'd give your AI tool, then describe how you'll review what it produces before using or sharing it.
5
What should the finished product look like?
Define the format, length, tone, and any content constraints. This is where you tell AI what good looks like.
6
Where could this go wrong?
Name two ways this workflow could produce a bad result — and write a rule to prevent each one.
